Hello! My name is Thomas Plunkett.
I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Tasmania, specialising in optical astronomy and planetary science.
My work focuses on the search for exoplanets with the transit and microlensing methods.

'The universe is a pretty big place. If it's just us, seems like an awful waste of space.' - Carl Sagan

Since the early 1990s, we have discovered nearly 6000 exoplanets across the Milky Way. This search has revealed a diverse 'zoo' of planets, ranging from infernal giants through to those reminiscent of Earth. Some are analogs of planets found in our own Solar System, whilst many are not so familiar. The ever expanding pursuit of new exoplanetary systems has forced us to questions the mechanisms involved in the formation and evolution of planets. This is the major motivation behind my research. By building a larger statistical sample of planets, we can begin to understand the processes that scultped their existence and therefore understand better how we ourselves 'arrived' on this pale, blue dot.
